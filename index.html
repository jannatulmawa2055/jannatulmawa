The Web Browser

History, Function, and its Role in the Modern Internet

Image Source: Internet

A web browser, often simply called a browser, is software that allows users to access information on the World Wide Web. More simply, a browser retrieves, presents, and traverses information resources on the internet. [1] Browsers retrieve data from web servers and translate HTML into text, images, videos, and buttons that users can interact with. Web browsers play an important role in daily life as most web-based applications are used today rely on browsers. Users learn, work, shop, watch videos, message friends, and do many other tasks through web browsers. While the internet would still exist without browsers, users would find it difficult to access most of the useful content.

Web browsers exist to simplify, organize, and enable regular people to use the internet. Websites can be very technical on their own, but browser companies take that code and display readable text on their browsers. Websites still function on their back end, but with browsers, users see neatly packaged pages along with an address bar, back and forward buttons, search, and tabs. Browser companies had major incentives to make browsing easy to use; competition drove companies to design better browsers and improve speed and features year after year. Competition also played a large role in what consumers expect a “modern browser” to look like. [2]
